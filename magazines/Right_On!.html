About Right On!

Right On! was published originally as a counterpoint to opinions in the Berkley Barb, one of many "underground papers" in the Berkeley, CA scene. This is cited as the first of the "Jesus papers", covering aspects of the 1960s and 1970s Jesus movement, followed closely by Agape (Seattle, WA).

The publication follows the evolution of the founding organization, Christian World Liberation Front. Early issues respond to local concerns (i.e. Berkeley Jesus Movement, street ministry concerns, local concert advertisements, etc.) with an evangelistic view. Later on, the paper more closely addresses national concerns, including the Miami political conventions of 1972 (October), and cult proliferation (see in 1974, Guru Maharaji). Their messaging shows concern for radical evangelism and discipleship, filtered through current events.

In 1973 the final issues of the Jesus Music oriented paper Rock In Jesus are integrated into the publication. Right On! continues regular coverage of the topic, provided by Paul Baker. Baker was a Dallas, Texas DJ who produced a syndicated radio show, A Joyful Noise, and later becomes editor of CCM.

CWLF split in 1975 and control of the paper stayed with staffer Sharon Gallagher. The name change the following year reflects this event, and completes the change in character of the paper from its radical roots. Radix takes on a mellower, more literary tone than Right On! reflected in its heyday.

For a detailed discussion of the time, place, people, and specifics of the CWLF, refer to the work of Donald Heinz.
